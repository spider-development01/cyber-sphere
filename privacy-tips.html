Privacy Tips to keep your data safe

To keep your data safe from hackers, here are some essential privacy tips based on the information from the sources provided:

  1. Encrypt Your Data:Set up encryption on your computer to safeguard personal information like data, texts, and emails.
  2. Use Secure Wi-Fi: Avoid using public Wi-Fi as it can put your personal data at risk. Always opt for a secure connection that requires a password.
  3. Create Strong Passwords: Choose complex passwords with a combination of lower and upper-case letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using easily guessable information like birthdays and change passwords periodically. Consider using a password manager tool to keep track of unique passwords.
  4. Monitor Activity: Regularly monitor activity across accounts, reports, and tools to detect any unusual behavior that could indicate a cyberattack. Utilize tools designed for monitoring user, device, and network activity.
  5. Backup Your Data: Backup your data regularly to prevent loss in case of device theft or loss. Cloud storage solutions are convenient options for backing up data securely.
  6. Update Devices:Always keep your devices up to date by installing the latest updates provided by operating systems like Microsoft and Apple. These updates often include security patches to protect against vulnerabilities.
  7. Be Cautious with File Sharing: Be selective about who has permission to view, comment, or edit your shared documents to avoid exposing sensitive information to unauthorized individuals.
  8. Install Security Software: Use antivirus software, anti-spyware software, and firewalls on all your devices for added protection against cyber threats. Consider cyber insurance for additional coverage in case of a cyberattack.

By following these privacy tips diligently, you can significantly enhance the security of your personal data and reduce the risk of falling victim to hackers or cybercriminals.

What are Cyber Crimes?

Cybercrimes are offences that may be committed against individuals, companies or institutions by using computers, internet or mobile technology. Cybercriminals use platforms such as social networking sites, emails, chatrooms, pirated software, websites, etc., to attack victims. Children are also vulnerable to various types of cybercrimes.

What is Cyber Bullying?

Cyberbullying refers to the act of using digital communication platforms, such as social media, to harass, intimidate, or harm others. It involves the use of technology to send or post hurtful messages, spread rumors, share inappropriate content, or engage in any form of online harassment. Cyberbullying can have serious emotional and psychological effects on the victims. It is important to report and address cyberbullying incidents promptly.

The consequences of cyber bullying on children can be physical, emotional and psychological consequences that can not only impact the academic performance of students but affect their daily life to a great extent.

How you can protect yourself from becoming a victim of Cyber Bullying?

  1. Don't accept friend requests from unknown people on social media platforms. Cyber bully can even create a fake account to befriend victims. As a rule of the thumb, only add people online whom you know offline.
  2. Don't share your personal information like date of birth, address, and phone number on social media or other online platforms. You can go to privacy settings on social media platforms to select who can access your posts online. Try to restrict access of your profile to your friends only. Picture stories for example on a particular platform are public by default.
  3. Remember what you post online remains there so it is important to be careful and not to share your phone number and other personal details in comments or posts on social media platforms.
  4. Never install unwanted Software and Apps like dating App, online games, etc. from unknown sources. You should be very careful while chatting in the chat rooms. Never share personal details in the chat room and limit your identity.
  5. If you feel hurt after reading a post from a friend or a stranger, don't react with aggressive reply. It may encourage the bully to keep posting such messages. If hurtful post/message is from your friend, you can request him not to do it again. If you are repeatedly getting such messages/ post, please inform your parents or elders immediately so that they can support you.
  6. Also, please remember that as a good citizen you should never share mean comments or hurtful messages or embarrassing pictures/videos online. Please be careful and check if your post/comment /videos can be embarrassing for your friend or anyone else. If so, please don't post. Types of Cybersecurity

The types of cybersecurity encompass various aspects of protecting organizations against cyber threats by securing different elements of their digital infrastructure.

  1. Network Security: Network security focuses on securing computer networks from unauthorized access, data breaches, and other network-based threats. It involves technologies like firewalls, intrusion detection systems, virtual private networks, and network segmentation.
  2. Application Security: Application security involves securing software applications from cyber-attacks by testing code, identifying vulnerabilities, and ensuring that applications are free from security flaws. It includes practices like code review, secure coding, and regular security testing.
  3. Information Security: Information security protects digital information stored in databases, files, or other repositories by ensuring confidentiality, integrity, and availability. It involves access control mechanisms, encryption of data, backups, disaster recovery plans, and monitoring system activity
  4. Cloud Security: Cloud security focuses on protecting data and systems hosted on cloud platforms like AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud. It includes technical and administrative controls to secure data stored in the cloud and the cloud infrastructure itself through secure configurations, access management controls, encryption, and regular audits.
  5. Endpoint Security: Endpoint security secures end-user devices such as desktops and laptops with data and network security controls to prevent threats like phishing and ransomware. It includes technologies for advanced threat prevention and endpoint detection and response solutions.
  6. Mobile Security: Mobile security protects tablets and smartphones that have access to corporate data from malicious apps, phishing attacks, and other threats. It secures operating systems and devices from rooting or jailbreaking to prevent unauthorized access.
  7. Data Security: Data security involves protecting sensitive information from unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. It includes encryption, access controls, data masking, and data loss prevention (DLP) techniques to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data stored or transmitted by an organization.
  8. Identity and Access Management (IAM):IAM involves managing user identities and controlling access to resources based on users' roles, permissions, and authentication credentials. It includes techniques such as multi-factor authentication (MFA), single sign-on (SSO), and privileged access management (PAM) to prevent unauthorized access and identity theft.

By implementing these types of cybersecurity measures, organizations can better protect their digital assets, information systems, and sensitive data from a wide range of cyber threats and attacks.

What you can do if you are a victim of Cyberbullying?

  1. Inform your parents/elders immediately: If someone is bullying you, you must inform your parents/elders immediately. Don't feel that your parents will restrict your online activity or ask you not to use your computer/smartphone. It is important to inform them so that they can support and guide you. Narrate the entire issue clearly to your parents/elders.
  2. Identify the bully: Try to identify if the bully is a known person or a stranger. You should try to find out the reason why bully is bothering you. A bully can be your friend or a known person. You may seek help of your parents/teachers to reach out to the bully and ask him/her to stop bullying you.
  3. Block the Bully: If bully is using social media platforms to bully you, you can block him/her. All the social media apps or services have the option to block a user.
  4. Collect and Save posts/messages: Save posts/messages that were used against you. Such messages/posts can be used as an evidence, if in case a legal action has to be taken.
  5. Never respond to a bully aggressively: Bully wants you to get aggressive and get into heated argument. This adds mileage to the information unwantedly. So the best way is to ask the person politely to stop it and if he/she becomes annoying, stop the chat/block him/her.
  6. Contact the police: If your parents/elders feel the need, they can contact the local police station to register a complaint against the bully.
